India's Super Computer

PARAM SIDDHI - AI is the fastest super computer of India, has a Rpeak of 5.67 petaflops and Rmax of 4.6 petaflops. The Supercomputer was developed by CDAC along with MeitY, build in 2015. It has secured the 63rd spot on the list of top 500 supercomputers in the world. Contains 42 NVIDIA DGX A100 systems.
PRATYUSH is world's 4th fastest supercomputer for climate and 78th in top 500 supercomputers. Developed by IITM pune in 2018. This a Cray-XC40 liquid cooled system. Running on Intel Xeon Broadwell E5-2695 processors. Has a system memory of 414 Terabytes and has a peak performance of 6.8 petaflops.
MIHIR is also built by IITM pune in year 2018. Bagged 120th position in list of top 500 supercomputers. Mihir has a maximum capacity of 2.5 petaflops. Mihir and Pratyush when clubbed together can achieve a peak performance as comparable to PARAM Siddhi-AI.
SAHASRAT is a Cray X-C40 developed by IISc Bengaluru. It is powered by an Intel Haswell 2.5 GHz CPU cluster with twelve cores, NVIDIA G40 4K graphic accelerator, 128 GB of RAM, 2.1 PB storage, and 1376 nodes. Secured 96th position world wide. Mainly used for aeronautical engineering and molecular research.
AADITYA is an IBM iDataplex supercomputer deployed at IITM Pune. Features Intel Sandy Bridge processors with 2384 compute nodes, 149 TB RAM, NVIDIA GF100GL graphic card, and 6 PB of storage, each node has 32 cores. AADITYA had earlier ranked 116th. Used to predict rainfall in smaller areas.
COLOR BLOSSOM is a Cray-X30 developed by TIFR Hyderabad. It runs on Linux and is powered by an Intel Xeon 5E-2680 processor, NVIDIA Tesla K20x graphic card, 1.1 PB of storage, and has a max speed of 558.7 TFlops. Color Blossom is to conduct research on quantum chromodynamics and physics.
PARAM YUVA-II is by CDAC along with intel and Netweb Technologies. In 2013 it secured 69th position in top 500. Gree500 listed YUVA-II on 44th position globally among the most power-efficient supercomputers with 1,760.20 megaflops speed. Powered by Intel Xeon - 5E-2670 with storage capacity of 200 TB.
PADUM is deployed in IIT Delhi. It is a graphics-focused, to perform graphics intense operations. Powered by NVIDIA's Tesla platform K40M gpu and two Intel Xeon G-6148 20 core CPU's clocked at 2.4 GHz. It is used for deep learning, data analytics, computational physics and many more.
VIRGO is based on IBM iDataplex dx360 M4 developed by IIT Madras in 2015. It features two intel E5-2670 processors clocked at 2.8Ghz, 64 GB RAM per node and a Mezzanine adapter. It has total of 292 compute nodes produces 97 TFlops.
PARAM Shivay is developed by CDAC and installed at IIT Varanasi in 2019. Its RND costs 32 crores. Has a maximum capacity of 833 Tflops. Powered by two Intel Skylake 6148 20 cores 2.4 GHz per node. Will be used in weather predictions and research in computational biology.
